## Course Description

SD 5009 is designed to equip designers with the critical-thinking skills needed to articulate creative ideas, concepts, and processes in an academic context. Participants learn a structured and systematic approach to becoming academic writers and design researchers.

Effective design research requires theoretical, practical, and methodological knowledge. Students learn how to organise research contexts, issues, and problems; formulate research and project questions; plan design and research processes; and communicate research outcomes. The course has two main objectives:

1. Identify research issues and problems by formulating appropriate academic research questions grounded in relevant empirical contexts.
2. Develop research outcomes with a logical flow that can be understood by different stakeholders.

The course moves through the foundations of academic writing: developing research interests, conducting literature reviews, identifying research gaps and suitable research questions, selecting rigorous research methodologies, analysing findings, and explaining implications. Students also learn to reconcile designers' abductive reasoning with researchers' inductive and deductive reasoning.

### Three Main Takeaways

1. Understand the basic content and structure of academic writing in design subjects.
2. Learn practical methods for transforming a design project into design research.
3. Improve critical and systemic thinking, logical flow, and the ability to work through language barriers.

### Learning Activities

- Lectures on fundamental academic writing knowledge and practice.
- Tutorial sessions that provide detailed support for developing writing skills.
- Mid-term and final presentations for sharing research progress and outcomes.
- Assigned readings, in-class exercises, and presentation activities that apply course knowledge and methods.

## Basic Rules and Guidelines for Course Work

These guidelines are intended to support a fair and efficient assessment process.

### Work Format

- The default format for assignments is group work, preferably in pairs of two students.
- Groups should align with students' capstone projects and normally use the same teams as their capstone cohort projects.
- Large capstone groups of four or five students should divide into two groups with slightly different research and writing focuses.
- Students are responsible for contributing equally and submitting work by the assigned deadline.
- Tutorials are held in MDes cohort studios.

### Use of Generative AI

Students should refer to the PolyU guidelines on the use of Generative Artificial Intelligence for all assignments. GenAI tools may help students produce high-quality text and improve writing skills, but the design researcher must ensure that their own voice, critical thinking, and research lead the academic writing.

### Mid-term Presentation

- The presentation should be clear, concise, and well organised.
- The verbal and digital presentation should use **5-7 slides**.
- It should cover the content of the first five classes and the initial stages of the Five Manners of research interest:
  1. **What:** topic and issue.
  2. **Why:** problems and motivations emerging from the literature review.
  3. **Focus:** research questions.

### Final Poster Presentation

- Present the project in poster format.
- Include the research question, methodology, findings, and conclusion.
- Deliver an oral presentation and answer questions from the audience.

## Poster Paper Guidelines

The course recommends using the [IASDR 2025 poster-paper guidelines](https://www.iasdr2025.org/submission-form.html#Poster-Papers) for reference.

- Poster papers may report work in progress or completed research.
- The paper should contain **2,500-3,000 words**, excluding the abstract and references.
- It should demonstrate a high degree of academic scholarship or practical significance.
- It should clearly articulate the research focus.
- It should provide a concise synthesis of the research context.
- It should describe the methods used to conduct the research.
- It should present the research findings and summarise the contribution to the field.
- The paper must be written in English.
- In-text citations and the reference list should follow **APA publication style**.
- The final file should be submitted as a PDF.
- Students may take inspiration from the IASDR template.
